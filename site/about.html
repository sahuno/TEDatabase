About L1-Somatic-DB

Last updated: —

Overview

L1-Somatic-DB is an automated, weekly-updated tracker of somatic LINE-1 (L1) retrotransposon insertions in human tissues and cancers, mapped to the hg38/GRCh38 reference genome. LINE-1 elements are the only autonomously active transposable elements in the human genome. Their somatic mobilization — particularly in cancer — has been implicated in genome instability, oncogene disruption, and tumor suppressor inactivation.

The database aggregates insertion coordinates, biological context, validation evidence, and bibliographic provenance from peer-reviewed literature and established community resources. All coordinates are reported in 0-based BED format against hg38.

Data Sources

L1-Somatic-DB integrates records from two primary sources:

  • PubMed literature (weekly search): Structured queries against NCBI PubMed retrieve newly indexed articles reporting somatic LINE-1 insertions. Full-text and abstract content is processed to extract genomic coordinates, tissue context, and validation methodology.
  • L1Base2 (seed database): L1Base2 provides a curated catalogue of full-length and 5'-truncated human L1 elements with activity annotations. L1Base2 records are used as the seed set for computationally predicted loci and are cross-referenced against literature reports to assign validation levels.

Methods and Pipeline

Data collection and processing follow a fully automated weekly pipeline:

  • Search: PubMed is queried weekly using controlled vocabulary terms (LINE-1, L1, somatic retrotransposition, somatic insertion, mobile element, cancer genome) combined with MeSH headings for human studies.
  • Download and parsing: Article metadata (title, abstract, journal, year, DOI, PMID) is retrieved via the NCBI Entrez API. Where open-access full text is available, it is fetched via PubMed Central.
  • LLM-assisted coordinate extraction: A large language model is prompted to extract genomic locus coordinates, tissue types, cancer types, detection methods, and validation statements from each article. Extracted coordinates are normalised to hg38 using liftOver where the source paper used an older genome build (hg19/GRCh37).
  • Coordinate validation: Extracted coordinates are cross-referenced against RepeatMasker LINE-1 annotations to confirm overlap with a known L1 element, and against L1Base2 to check for prior curation. Coordinates that do not overlap any RepeatMasker L1 feature are flagged with coordinate_confidence = low.
  • Weekly update: New records passing quality filters are appended to the database. Existing records are updated with newly published validation evidence. stats.json is regenerated on each run.

Evidence Levels

Every locus is assigned one of three validation levels reflecting the strength of evidence for the insertion being a genuine somatic retrotransposition event:

Level Badge Criteria Typical methods
Experimental experimental The insertion has been independently confirmed by a wet-lab assay in the study reporting it. Evidence for somatic origin (absent in matched normal or germline) is documented. PCR validation, Sanger sequencing, long-read validation, Southern blot
Computational computational The insertion was detected by a computational pipeline applied to sequencing data (WGS, WES, or RNA-seq) and reported in a peer-reviewed study, but without an independent wet-lab confirmation step. MELT, RetroSeq, TLDR, TraFiC, L1EM, Mobster, nanopore SV callers
Predicted predicted The locus is derived from L1Base2 activity annotations or from LLM-extracted coordinates that could not be independently cross-referenced to a primary source. Treat with caution; coordinates may be approximate. L1Base2 annotation, LLM extraction without cross-reference

Coordinate Confidence

Separate from the evidence level, coordinate confidence reflects the positional precision of the reported insertion site:

  • High — exact breakpoint resolved to base-pair resolution; coordinates overlap a RepeatMasker LINE-1 feature; liftOver completed successfully if a build conversion was required.
  • Medium — coordinates overlap a RepeatMasker LINE-1 feature but positional precision is limited (e.g., reported as a cytogenetic band or kilobase-resolution window).
  • Low — coordinates could not be validated against a RepeatMasker feature, liftOver failed or was incomplete, or the original publication did not report a precise genomic position.

Genome Build

All coordinates in L1-Somatic-DB are reported in hg38 (GRCh38), 0-based half-open (BED) format. Coordinates originally reported against hg19/GRCh37 were converted using UCSC liftOver with the hg19ToHg38.over.chain file. Unconverted loci are excluded from the current release.

Limitations

  • LLM-assisted extraction is not error-free. Extracted coordinates from complex figure legends or supplementary tables may be imprecise. Always verify against the primary source before using individual loci for mechanistic studies.
  • Coverage is biased toward cancer types with large published WGS cohorts (colorectal, liver, lung, breast). Rare tumour types and normal tissue insertions are underrepresented.
  • The database captures only insertions reported in indexed PubMed literature and L1Base2. Novel insertions in preprints or conference proceedings are not included until publication.
